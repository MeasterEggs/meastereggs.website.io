Quick tip

There are 12 pitches in an octave — 7 natural notes plus 5 sharps/flats. Every sharp has an enharmonic flat twin (same pitch, different name). The French system uses solfège syllables and is standard in France, Switzerland, Spain, and Italy. Si is sometimes written Ti in English-speaking countries.

Major modes — brighter, more resolved
Ionian (major)
Mode I · W W H W W W H
Bright, happy, resolved. The most "neutral" and optimistic sound in Western music.
joyful uplifting stable
Lydian
Mode IV · W W W H W W H
Dreamy, ethereal, floating. Major with a raised 4th — adds wonder and magic.
dreamy magical otherworldly
Mixolydian
Mode V · W W H W W H W
Bright but slightly unresolved. Major feel with a bluesy, rock edge from the flat 7th.
energetic bluesy triumphant
Minor modes — darker, more expressive
Aeolian (natural minor)
Mode VI · W H W W H W W
Sad, melancholic, introspective. The most common minor sound — heavy but familiar.
sad melancholic dark
Dorian
Mode II · W H W W W H W
Minor but with a hopeful quality. Sad yet sophisticated — jazz, soul, and folk love this one.
soulful bittersweet groovy
Phrygian
Mode III · H W W W H W W
Dark, tense, Spanish-flavored. The flat 2nd gives it an exotic, almost menacing quality.
tense exotic flamenco
Locrian
Mode VII · H W W H W W W
Unstable, dissonant, eerie. The only mode with a diminished tonic — rarely used melodically.
dissonant unsettling horror
Non-diatonic scales
Harmonic minor
Natural minor + raised 7th
Dramatic, classical, Middle-Eastern. The raised 7th creates a yearning, exotic tension.
dramatic yearning cinematic
Pentatonic minor
5-note · 1 ♭3 4 5 ♭7
Raw, emotive, universal. Blues, rock, and folk staple — hard to play a wrong note.
bluesy raw rock
Pentatonic major
5-note · 1 2 3 5 6
Open, folk-like, timeless. Happy and simple — country, pop, and world music all use it.
folk open timeless
Whole tone
6-note · all whole steps
Ambiguous, floating, impressionistic. No sense of resolution — used for dreamlike passages.
ambiguous floating impressionist

W = whole step (2 semitones) · H = half step (1 semitone). The seven church modes all share the same parent major scale — just with a different starting degree.
